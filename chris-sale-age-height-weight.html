    Chris Sale Age, Height, Weight: Chris Sale, officially known as Christopher Allen Sale is an American professional baseball pitcher born on March 30, 1989.

    He developed a love for baseball at a younger age and has been consistent throughout his career rising through the ranks to become one of the most sought-after baseball players (a pitcher).

    Sale played college baseball for Florida Gulf Coast University and at the time of this writing (Sunday, April 2, 2023), he was a baseball pitcher for the Boston Red Sox of Major League Baseball (MLB).

    He previously played in Major League Baseball for the Chicago White Sox, with whom he made his major league debut on Friday, August 6, 2010.

    The Chicago White Sox selected him 13th overall in the 2010 amateur draft and was called up to the majors for the first time on August 4, 2010, where he made his MLB debut on August 6, of the same year.

    On December 6, 2016, the Chicago White Sox traded Sale to the Boston Red Sox. Upon joining Boston, he switched his uniform number from 49 to 41 because 49 is unofficially retired for Tim Wakefield.

    Chris Sale was a key member of the Red Sox pitching rotation that helped the team win the 2018 World Series.

    He’s a seven-time MLB All-Star from 2012 to 2018, and a two-time American League (AL) leader in strikeouts, including in 2015 and 2017.

    As of 2022, he is MLB’s career leader in strikeout-to-walk ratio (5.33) and reached 2,000 strikeouts in the fewest innings pitched (1,626).

    After reaching 300 strikeouts in a single season for the first time in 2017, he was selected as the AL Sporting News Starting Pitcher of the Year.

    As a power pitcher, Sale excels at achieving high numbers of strikeouts and has set many strikeout-related records.
