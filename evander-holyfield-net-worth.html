    Evander Holyfield | Net Worth and Income

    Multi-talented athlete and boxer Evander Holyfield’s net worth is $1 million as of December 2023.

    In 1996, ticket sales of Holyfield’s fight against Mike Tyson was around $14,150,000. In addition, they generated approximately 1.59 million buys for $79,000,000.

    Financial Trouble

    Evander amassed a net worth of roughly $230 million over the course of his career after accounting for inflation.

    Unfortunately, by 2008, all of that money had been spent.

    Excessive spending, several unsuccessful business enterprises, ongoing alimony, and his three broken marriages, among other things, destroyed his money.

    Despite earning $230 million during his boxing career, Holyfield was described as “flat penniless and broke” by The Independent in 2012.

    Evander managed to blow through a wealth that peaked at over $200 million at one point.

    The mother of his then 10-year-old kid filed a lawsuit against him for non-payment of two months of child support (he was paying $3,000 a month for the child).

    A landscaping company in Utah has also gone to court to demand $550,000 in outstanding debt for services.

    He also owed the IRS several hundred thousand dollars in overdue taxes as of January 2013.

    Most of Holyfield’s valued things, including his Olympic bronze medal, 20 pairs of fight gloves, national championships, belts, and 25 match robes and shorts, have been sold at auction throughout the years to meet a variety of debts and responsibilities.

    Although, he is slowly recovering from the losses and making a decent living today.

    Furthermore, Holyfield was making around $1.2 million per year as of 2019, largely from personal engagements.

    Evander Holyfield | Lifestyle and Vacations

    Lifestyle

    Holyfield was born in the mill town of Atmore, Alabama, on October 19, 1962.

    Evander was the youngest of nine children and was born to a different father than his other siblings.

    His family eventually relocated to Atlanta, Georgia, where he grew up in the dangerous Bowen Homes Housing Projects.

    Holyfield began boxing and won the Boys Club boxing event at seven. Similarly, he qualified for his first Junior Olympics when he was thirteen.

    By the age of 15, Holyfield had won the Southeastern Regional Championship and the Best Boxer Award.

    He had a history of 160 wins and 14 losses by 1984, with 76 victories coming via knockout.

    Holyfield considers himself a physical “late starter,” as he was only 5 feet 8 inches tall when he graduated from Fulton High School in 1980.

    However, he had reached 6 feet by the age of 21, and in his early twenties, he had grown another two and a half inches.

    From 1985 to 1991, Holyfield was married to Paulette Brown. 

    In 1996, he married Dr. Janice Itson. They divorced in 2000 after having one kid together.

    From 2003 through 2012, Evander was married to Candi Calvana Smith.

    In fact, Holyfield was married to six different women and had eleven children.

    Evan, one of his sons, went on to become a professional boxer, following in his father’s footsteps.

    His other son, Elijah, is a college football player.

    Evander was connected to Applied Pharmacy Services, which was being investigated in Alabama for supplying athletes with illicit steroids.

    Although, in 2007, he denied ever using performance-enhancing drugs.

    Vacation

    Evander has traveled the world in his long career. Mostly, he spends his time in the gym training athletes, but he loves to spend quality time with children.

    Holyfield likes to relax beside a swimming pool or beach when he isn’t training.

    Moreover, his favorite vacation spot is Florida.

    Evander Holyfield | Career

    In 1984, Holyfield made his professional debut as a light heavyweight with a televised six-round victory over Lionel Byarm at Madison Square Garden.

    On January 20, 1985, he defeated Eric Winbush in a six-round decision in Atlantic City, New Jersey.

    In Norfolk, Virginia, on March 13, he knocked out Fred Brown in the opening round, and in Corpus Christi, Texas, on April 20, he beat out Mark Rivera in two rounds.

    Holyfield didn’t lose a professional fight until 1992, when Riddick Bowe defeated him. 

    He reclaimed the championship in a rematch with Bowe a year later, winning the WBA and IBF titles, but lost them in an upset to Michael Moorer in 1994.

    Similarly, he defeated boxing legend Mike Tyson in a match in 1997. During the game, Tyson bit off Holyfield’s ear and was disqualified.

    At 45, he decided to retire, and he was enlisted into the International Boxing Hall of Fame in 2017.
