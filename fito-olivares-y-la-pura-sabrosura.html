    Overall Meaning

    In Fito Olivares Y La Pura Sabrosura's song "La Gallina," the lyrics describe a chicken that is constantly making noise, clucking and laying eggs. The imagery of the chicken is often interpreted as a metaphor for a woman who is always talking and making a fuss. The repetition of the line "mi gallina siempre está cacaraqueando" emphasizes this idea and portrays a humorous snapshot of the daily life of a chicken. The line "pone un huevo y luego está cacaraqueando" also adds to the comedic aspect of the song, as it presents a commentary on the frivolous nature of certain habits.


    The use of "música" in between the stanzas signals a break in the song, allowing for instrumental interludes to highlight the saxophone-heavy melody of the cumbia genre. Additionally, the inclusion of the line "(que se oiga hasta el gallinero primo)" further emphasizes the intended lightheartedness of "La Gallina," prompting the listener to imagine a party atmosphere where all individuals, including chickens, are welcome to cluck and dance to the rhythm of the beat.
