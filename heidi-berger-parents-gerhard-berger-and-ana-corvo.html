    Meet Heidi Berger Dad, Gerhard Berger

    Gerhard Berger, the dad of Heidi Berger, is Austrian by nationality.

    • Gerhard Berger Age

    The date of birth of Gerhard Berger is 27 August 1959. That makes him 62 years old in 2022.

    • Gerhard Berger Job

    Gerhard Berger is a former driver in Formula One from 1984 to 1997. Berger is one of the most seasoned drivers of all time with 210 starts in Grand Prix. He secured 10 wins. Blooded in the high-stress political milieu of the Ferrari team in the middle of the 1980s, he subsequently had the difficult task of replacing Alain Prost as the renowned Ayrton Senna’s teammate on the McLaren-Honda team at the beginning of 1990.

    This Austrian haulage contractor’s son, who had raced in F3 and toured cars before, entered Formula One in the summer of 1984 while operating an ATS-BMW. He didn’t finish his first race in front of his home fans at the Osterreichring, but he followed it up with a strong sixth place at Monza. However, he was not eligible to get the championship point that normally went along with that finish because he wasn’t a qualified title contender.

    Gerhard raced for the Arrows-BMW team in 1985 before switching to the fledgling Benetton-BMW team in 1986. He won the Mexican Grand Prix and was awarded a three-year Ferrari deal as a result of that victory and several other consistently strong efforts. He left Formula One in 1997, and from 2000 to 2003 he served as the Competitions Director for the BMW Formula One engine supplier.

    Gerhard won the Australian GP in Adelaide and the Japanese GP at Suzuka in 1987. In 1988, he became the lone non-McLaren driver to win a race when he beat teammate Michele Alboreto in a fortuitous triumph at Monza.

    Before joining McLaren and spending three years attempting to accept Ayrton Senna as his teammate, he had a disastrous year in 1989, now paired with Nigel Mansell. Berger grew to be one of Ayrton’s closest friends and was never shy about giving the Brazilian his take on anything. It was never going to be simply working with a talent like Senna, but Berger did his best.

    Later on, Gerhard stated in 2006 that he had acquired a 50% share in the brand-new Toro Rosso Formula One team not long after leaving BMW. At the end of 2008, Berger repurchased his ownership stake from co-owner Dietrich Mateschitz.

    In addition, he assumed the role of President of the FIA Single Seat Commission in 2012 and held it until December 2014. Additionally, he presently serves as chair of ITR, the organization that organizes the Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters.

    Fun Fact: A Ferrari Testarossa originally supplied to Formula 1 driver Gerhard Berger while he was driving for the Scuderia was auctioned in Monaco in May 2022. Chassis no. ZFFSA17S000080974 was delivered to Berger via the official Ferrari dealership Denzel in Innsbruck, Austria in May 1989, ahead of his final season racing for Ferrari.
