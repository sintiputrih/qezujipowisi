    A Pokémon card that is worth $1 million is the rare holographic "Illustrator" Pikachu card. This card was first released in 1998 and has become the most expensive Pokémon card to date. The value of ultra rare Pokémon cards can range from thousands to millions of dollars due to factors such as rarity, cultural significance, market forces, online marketplaces, quality, condition, and the enduring popularity of the Pokémon franchise.

    The Pikachu Illustrator card is considered the rarest and most expensive Pokémon card, with prices ranging from $375,000 to $6 million. It is highly sought after by collectors and is incredibly hard to find. This card features unique symbols and was originally only given to winners of Pokémon illustration contests in Japan. Its value has skyrocketed over the years, with a recent record-breaking sale of $900,000.

    Other valuable ultra rare Pokémon cards include the 1999 Pokemon Game Charizard-Holo First Edition card, the Tsunekazu Ishihara SM Black Star promo card, the 1998 Pokemon Japanese Promo Kangaskhan-Holo Family Event Trophy Card, and the 2000 Pokemon Neo Genesis 1st Edition Lugia-Holo card. These cards also hold significant value in the Pokémon card collecting community.

    In recent years, the value of Pokémon cards has experienced a significant increase. Some cards have seen a 500% increase in value since 2019. This surge in value can be attributed to various factors, including the growing popularity of Pokémon as a franchise, increased demand from collectors, limited supply, and nostalgia. As a result, collectors and investors are willing to pay top dollar for rare and valuable Pokémon cards.

    The Pokémon card market operates similarly to other collectibles markets, with prices being determined by supply and demand dynamics. Online marketplaces have made it easier for collectors to buy and sell Pokémon cards, further fueling the market. However, the high value of these cards also makes them a target for counterfeiters, so it is important for collectors to authenticate the cards they purchase.

    When it comes to investing in Pokémon cards, it is crucial to consider factors such as the card's condition, rarity, and long-term potential. Mint condition cards with high rarity are generally more valuable and have a greater chance of appreciating in value over time.
