    Xavi Hernández Biography

    Born January 25, 1980, he is widely regarded as one of the greatest midfielders in the history of the sport, Xavi earned recognition for his exceptional passing, visionary play, ball retention, and strategic positioning. Throughout his illustrious career, he secured 32 trophies, making him the second-most decorated Spanish player, trailing only his former teammate Andrés Iniesta.

    Xavi’s impressive list of accolades includes six appearances in the FIFA FIFPro World XI from 2008 to 2013 and five selections to the UEFA Team of the Year from 2008 to 2012. In 2020, he was honored with a place in the Ballon d’Or Dream Team, a compilation of the greatest players of all time by France Football. Notably, Xavi is among the few players to have surpassed 1,000 professional career appearances.

    Beginning his football journey at Barcelona’s youth academy, La Masia, at the age of 11, Xavi made his debut for the first team at 18 in August 1998. His remarkable contribution includes 767 official matches and 85 goals, establishing him as a club legend. Xavi also holds the distinction of being the first player in Barcelona’s history to participate in 150 European and FIFA Club World Cup matches combined.

    During his tenure with Barcelona, Xavi clinched eight La Liga titles and four UEFA Champions League titles. In 2015, he moved to Al Sadd, where he secured four additional trophies before retiring in 2019. Internationally, Xavi played a pivotal role in Spain’s triumphs at the FIFA World Cup in 2010, UEFA Euro 2008, and UEFA Euro 2012. He announced his retirement from international football after the 2014 FIFA World Cup.

    Xavi’s impact goes beyond his playing days, as he transitioned to coaching. After winning seven titles in less than three years with Al Sadd, he returned to Barcelona in November 2021 as the manager. In his first full season, he led the team to victory in the 2022–23 Supercopa de España and the 2022–23 La Liga title.

    Known for his ability to find and exploit space as a deep-lying playmaker, Xavi’s playing style focused on intelligent ball distribution and maintaining possession. Despite lacking pace and physicality, his technical skills, vision, and ball control made him a maestro on the field. Nicknamed “The Puppet Master,” Xavi’s influence extended beyond his playing ability, earning praise for his leadership qualities.

    In his coaching role, Xavi continues to adhere to the principles of the Barcelona style, drawing inspiration from his experiences with coaches like Johan Cruyff and Pep Guardiola. He emphasizes the importance of managing the dressing room, blending coaching and psychological aspects to create a cohesive team.

    Off the field, Xavi has been associated with Adidas and participated in various campaigns, including FIFA’s “11 against Ebola” initiative in 2014, where he joined other top football players to raise awareness about the disease.
